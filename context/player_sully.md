# Sam "Sully" Sullivan: The Fool and the King

## Looks
Sam is a broad-shouldered man in his late 30s with a ruddy complexion, an expressive brow, and short, reddish hair. He usually wears casual graphic tees under unbuttoned flannel shirts, looking like he just stepped off a casual weekend hike.

## Style of Play
Sam is a master of physical comedy and quick-witted punchlines, but he is also the show's secret weapon for drama. He plays characters who seem like goofy comic relief on the surface—cowardly sidekicks, eccentric wizards, or dim-witted bards—but who possess deeply hidden depths of trauma or nobility. Sam loves the classic clown’s transition from comedy to tragedy; he will make the table laugh for twenty minutes, only to pivot on a dime and deliver a line of quiet, crushing sorrow that catches everyone completely off guard.

## Mannerisms
Sam uses his entire upper body to act out his scenes. He will slump over the table when his character is exhausted, or puff out his chest when pretending to be a pompous noble. He has a booming, infectious laugh that fills the studio, but during serious moments, he drops into a completely deadpan, motionless stare, letting the silence do the work.
